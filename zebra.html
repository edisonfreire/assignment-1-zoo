Zebra

Zebras are strikingly beautiful animals known for their distinctive black-and-white striped coats, which serve as their most recognizable feature. These stripes are unique to each individual, much like human fingerprints, and are believed to help with camouflage, confuse predators, and regulate body temperature. Zebras are social animals that live in herds, often led by a dominant stallion, and are found primarily in the grasslands and savannas of Africa. They are herbivores, feeding mainly on grasses, and their strong, sturdy teeth are well-adapted to grazing. Despite their peaceful appearance, zebras can be aggressive when threatened, using powerful kicks and bites to defend themselves. Their social structure and strong sense of community help them survive in the wild, where they face natural predators like lions and hyenas.
