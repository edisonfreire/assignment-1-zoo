Lion

Lions, often referred to as the "king of the jungle," are one of the most iconic and majestic animals in the animal kingdom. They are the only big cats that live in social groups called prides, which typically consist of related females, their offspring, and a small number of adult males. Male lions are distinguished by their impressive manes, which vary in color and size and serve as a sign of strength and dominance. Lions are apex predators, primarily hunting large herbivores such as zebras, wildebeests, and buffaloes. They are known for their strength, powerful jaws, and cooperative hunting tactics, which often involve teamwork to bring down large prey. Despite their fearsome reputation, lions spend much of their time resting and are known to sleep for up to 20 hours a day. Found mainly in sub-Saharan Africa, lions are symbols of courage and nobility, though their populations are declining due to habitat loss and human-wildlife conflict.
